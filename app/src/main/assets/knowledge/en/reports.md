# Reports

## Overview

The Reports section gives you a bird's-eye view of your business performance. You can view sales summaries, profit margins, inventory movement, and customer activity all from one place.

## Accessing Reports

Open the bottom navigation bar and tap **Insights**. The Insights screen contains several tabs:

- **Today** — today's running totals (sales, profit, transactions)
- **Trends** — week-over-week and month-over-month charts
- **Inventory** — stock movement and low-stock items

## Sales Reports

The Sales report shows every transaction in a selected date range. To filter:

1. Tap the calendar icon at the top of the screen.
2. Choose a start date and end date.
3. Tap **Apply** to reload the data.

The summary card at the top shows:
- **Total revenue** — sum of all sale amounts in the period
- **Transaction count** — how many sales were made
- **Average sale value** — revenue ÷ transaction count

## Profit Reports

Profit is calculated as revenue minus cost of goods sold (COGS). The app uses the cost price you set for each product. If cost prices are missing, profit will be underreported.

To improve accuracy, make sure every product has a **Cost price** entered in its edit screen.

## Inventory Reports

The Inventory section shows:
- Current stock levels for all products
- Items flagged as low stock (below your set threshold)
- Products with zero stock

Tap any product row to jump to that product's edit screen and update its stock or price.

## Exporting Data

Long-press on any report card to see export options (where available). You can share report data as plain text or via your phone's share sheet.

## Daily Summary

At end of day, use **Sales → menu → Close day** to generate a daily summary. This shows the day's totals, top-selling products, and a cash reconciliation section.

## Tips

- Check the Trends tab weekly to spot growth or decline early.
- Low-margin products show in orange in the profit breakdown — consider raising prices or reducing costs.
- The AI Chat assistant can answer questions like "What were my best-selling products last week?" for quick verbal reports.
