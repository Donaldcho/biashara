# Daily Cash Count
feature_id: cash_count
language: en

The Cash Count feature helps you verify that the physical cash in your till matches what the app recorded for the day. It is a key daily discipline to catch discrepancies early.

## When to Do a Cash Count

Do a cash count at the **end of each business day** before closing, or at any shift change if you have multiple staff.

## How to Perform a Cash Count

1. Go to **Ledger → Cash Count** or tap **End of Day** from the home screen.
2. The app shows the **Expected Cash Balance**: the opening float plus all cash sales for the day minus cash refunds.
3. Count your physical cash. You can use the **bill counter** on screen — tap each denomination and enter how many notes/coins you have:
   - KES 1000, 500, 200, 100, 50, 40, 20, 10, 5, 1 (for Kenya)
   - TZS denominations if Tanzania is selected
4. The app calculates the **Actual Cash Total** from your entries.
5. The **Difference** is displayed:
   - **0**: Perfect match.
   - **Positive (surplus)**: You have more cash than expected.
   - **Negative (shortage)**: You have less cash than expected.
6. Add a **Note** to explain any difference (e.g., "KES 200 given for cleaning supplies not recorded").
7. Tap **Close Day** to save the cash count record and reset for the next day.

## Viewing Past Cash Counts

Go to **Ledger → Cash Count History** to see each day's opening balance, expected total, actual total, and difference.

## What Causes Differences?

- Unrecorded sales (someone sold without ringing it up)
- Cash given as change incorrectly
- Cash taken for expenses without recording
- Counting errors

## Opening Float

Set your opening float (starting cash in the till) in **Settings → Cash Count → Default Opening Float**.
